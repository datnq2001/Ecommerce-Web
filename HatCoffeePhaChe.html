ART OF MAKING COFFEE

Until today, phin-brewed coffee is still considered by coffee connoisseurs as one of the best ways to brew coffee due to its ability to preserve the full flavor of freshly ground coffee beans.

Phin-brewed coffee is made by placing an appropriate amount of coffee grounds into the phin, pressing lightly with a lid, adding a measured amount of hot water, and allowing the brewing process to occur. The hot water gradually filters through the coffee grounds, extracting the coffee's flavors slowly to achieve the perfect extraction.

A good phin-brewed coffee usually involves two main factors: firstly, it requires keeping the hot water tightly sealed and for a long time during the brewing process. Secondly, the coffee needs a bit of time to "bloom" by pouring a little hot water into the coffee phin (not too much to the extent that coffee drops can drip down). This allows the delightful flavors in the coffee beans to fully blend.

The moment each coffee drop falls from the phin allows us to appreciate the value of waiting. Savoring the rich traditional coffee flavor along with the moments of watching each drop, each coffee drop falling is also a peaceful moment that helps you regain balance amidst the rush of life.
