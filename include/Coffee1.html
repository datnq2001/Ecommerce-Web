The darker the coffee is roasted, the more complete the flavor becomes. Roasting coffee is a process that demands delicacy from the hands and an understanding of each type of coffee bean from the artisan. Small fires during roasting leave the coffee underdeveloped and more bitter, while over-roasting leads to burnt flavors, losing the superior qualities it originally possessed. Throughout the roasting process, nutrients like proteins and enzymes accumulate inside, forming the essence of the coffee, making it richer and smoother.

On the quest for the finest coffee beans, Phuc Long always emphasizes four characteristics from the coffee cherries to respect the originality for a bold-flavored coffee brew.
- Aroma is the scent of coffee beans - the more aroma, the more promise for various flavors.
- Body refers to the richness in the coffee extract. It is felt through the evaluation of the taster.
- Acidity is the compound that creates the bright, tangy taste of coffee.
- Aftertaste is the lingering sensation of coffee flavor after consumption.

The perfect cup of coffee is defined as one with a strong, bitter taste, a smooth acidity, and a lingering, intense aroma that easily captivates anyone's taste buds. Bold-flavored coffee has always held a certain position in the hearts of Vietnamese coffee enthusiasts, despite changes in the culture of coffee consumption as society evolves day by day.
