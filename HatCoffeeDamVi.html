JOURNEY OF STRONG FLAVOR COFFEE

The strong flavor coffee you are enjoying is the result of Phuc Long's relentless efforts over more than half a century. Few people know that the coffee processing process requires very high precision. Any neglected link in the processing process can affect the entire chain, from cultivation to pest control measures, to harvesting, processing, transportation, roasting, grinding, packaging, and finally brewing strong flavor coffee.

Purity from the source

Nurturing purity in every cup of coffee, Phuc Long has continuously sought and selected the best Arabica and Robusta coffee varieties. Pioneering in providing freshly packaged coffee at the place of harvest to the hands of consumers. We know that the quality of the first coffee is determined by three core factors.

Preserve the superior characteristics of coffee beans

Coffee Cherry is also known as Jackfruit Coffee. Cherry coffee beans have a beautiful yellow, shiny color. Although the taste is not as rich as Robusta, nor as aromatic as Arabica, Cherry coffee beans have their own characteristics: slightly sour, slightly bitter, low caffeine content, with a faint fragrance.

Respect the original for strong flavor coffee

Moka coffee is a very difficult coffee variety to grow, belonging to the Arabica genus, widely grown in Da Lat, Lam Dong, especially Moka Cau Dat. In Vietnam, Moka coffee is classified as a truly rare product due to its excellent quality and taste: elegant fragrance, refreshing sourness.
